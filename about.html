About

The numbers were never about prediction. They were about people.

Numbers don't lie. The day you were born says something true about who you are — about who you belong with, and whose energy you match.

How it started

I spent years studying patterns in behaviour — in people, in myself, in the numbers that kept describing both. What I kept noticing wasn't the mysticism. It was the accuracy. Over and over, a person's birthday described them more honestly than they could describe themselves.

So I did the work of turning what I'd learned into a system of my own — clear enough to write down, and precise enough to trust. That became The Personality Code and The Relationship Code.

“Your date of birth is a code. Once you can read it, people stop being a mystery.”

What I believe

Most of the world runs on information — more advice, more content, more noise. I've never believed information changes anyone. Acceptance does. Accepting yourself and your current state changes you. The moment you finally get a confirmation of who you are, something shifts — and you start walking the path you were always meant to be on.

That's the whole point of the numbers for me. Not to predict your future, but to confirm what's already true — to make you and the people in your life legible: understandable, forgivable, reachable.

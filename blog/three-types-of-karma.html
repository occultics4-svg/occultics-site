The Three Types of Karma in Your Birth Date

Published by Occultics · 2026

In the Pythagorean system, karma is not a vague spiritual concept. It is a specific mathematical pattern encoded in your birth date. It shows up as numbers — and those numbers tell you exactly what kind of karmic weight you carry, where it comes from, and how long it will take to resolve.

There are three types of karma. Each one is tied to a specific number. And most people carry at least one of them.

The three karmic numbers

Inherited Karma
4

The 4 carries karma inherited from your ancestors. This is not something you created — it is something passed down through your family line. The weight of decisions made generations before you lands on your shoulders. For people who carry the 4, the greatest obstacles in life are often tied to their place of birth. The research shows something remarkable: the farther you move from your birthplace, the less influence this inherited karma has. The 4 tests you through hard work, delays, and limitations. Nothing comes easy. But the 4 also rewards persistence — when you break through, what you build is unshakable and lasting.

Personal Karma
6

The 6 carries the greatest personal karma. This is karma you created yourself — in a past life. According to the Pythagorean tradition, every 6 carries the weight of having taken a life in a previous existence. In this life, they compensate by giving everything they have. Sixes sacrifice themselves for the people they love, often to the point of forgetting about themselves entirely. Their karma is resolved through love, family, and emotional relationships — these are the arenas where they must grow, where they are tested, and where they ultimately find fulfillment. But the 6 is also the number of beauty, talent, and Venus. The karma is not only burden — it is also gift.

Karmic Repayment
9

The 9 is karma being repaid. When the 9 appears in your code or in a compatibility code, it signals that a debt from a past life is being settled — now. Relationships with a 9 feel fated because they are. Two people with a 9 in their synastry were destined to meet, fulfill a karmic task, and then — in many cases — part ways. The 9 is also the number of secrets, hidden agendas, and things left unsaid. Communication is limited. Emotions are kept private. And when the karmic lesson has been learned, the connection dissolves as naturally as it formed.

A real example — born January 1, 1977

Let's take a woman born on January 1, 1977. Her code is:

Her code
2 . 6 . 8
Biorhythm 2 · Generational 6 · Life Path 8

She carries a 1 in her day of birth and an 8 as her Life Path. The 1 is the Sun — ambition, leadership, the drive to be first. The 8 is Mars — struggle, competition, vitality, and the relentless pursuit of material success. With her 8-8 degrees and a day birth, both the 1 and the 8 are fully active. The masculine energy is fully expressed. She will rise. She will achieve. This is mathematical certainty.

Her Biorhythm 2 adds a fated quality to her life — things happen to her that feel destined, not chosen.

The karmic tension in her code sits between the 8's relentless drive toward power and the demands of her relationships. The 1 in her birth date says she must lead — and she will. But her code says that if she neglects the people around her in pursuit of success, something will always feel incomplete.

How karma shows up in relationships
When two people meet, their codes combine to produce a compatibility code. If that code contains a 4, a 6, or a 9, a form of karma is present in the relationship. A 4 in the compatibility code means the couple must endure karmic trials together — often related to finances, children, or family circumstances. A 6 means the relationship itself is a karmic arena — love, betrayal, sacrifice, and growth are all part of the contract. A 9 means the relationship has an expiration date — the karma will be repaid, the lesson will be learned, and then the connection ends. Knowing which karma you carry does not remove it. But it tells you what you are working with — and that changes everything.

The karmic paradox

Karma in the Pythagorean system is not punishment. It is a task. The 4 does not punish you with hard work — it gives you the opportunity to build something that lasts beyond your lifetime. The 6 does not punish you with emotional pain — it teaches you to love so deeply that you repay what was taken in another life. The 9 does not punish you with endings — it frees you from a debt so you can begin again.

The woman born on January 1, 1977 will succeed in her career. The 8 guarantees it. The 1 in her birth date guarantees she will lead. But her code also says that her deepest growth — the work her soul came here to do — happens not in the boardroom but in the relationships she builds and the people she chooses to keep close.

That is karma. And it is both her greatest challenge and her greatest gift.

What karma do you carry?

Look at your code. If you carry a 4 — in your Biorhythm, Generational, or Life Path number — you carry inherited karma from your ancestors. If you carry a 6, your karma is personal — tied to love, relationships, and what you must give back. If you carry a 9, you are in a period of repayment — learning lessons that will free you for what comes next.

And if your compatibility code with someone contains these numbers, the relationship is not random. It is karmic. It was always going to happen.
